About Me

My full name is José Jorge Rodríguez Salgado, and I'm Cuban. I studied CS in University of Havana. After getting my bachelor, I started to teach Probabilities and Statistics in the same faculty I got my degree. I have been researching since then, focused in the Vehicle Routing Problem. I also have made a living from developing websites, software testing and software development in general. Although I don't call myself a developer, I think I have some experiences that can help some people in this field. 

I love sports, play the guitar and, off course, CS. But I think I love debates about CS the most. That's the main reason I thought about starting a blog. But that is not the only reason. First of all I love teaching, that feeling of being in the front of a classroom is incredible. But there are lot of channels to share my thoughts and my experiences and this blog is one of them. Whenever I google some CS-related topic and read good or bad articles I always feel that I want say something different, with this blog I am trying to be read and I hope that you find good content here

I'd like you to follow me on Twitter. I'm very interested in creating a community and all of us to grow together. I will be opening some threads and debates about CS topics in Twitter as well. Check the bottom of every page in this blog and you'll find the link. Enjoy!
